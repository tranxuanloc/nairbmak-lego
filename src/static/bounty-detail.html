Creative use of Ohmni Arm & the Ohmni Dev Kit

Are you a maker? Do you want to do something that is innovative & matter? At Ohmni Lab, we just announced an open development kit for telepresence robotics to enable faster, easier, and less expensive development of customized robotic applications that sit on top of telepresence systems. And guess what? This mission need you!

Take up the Challenge

and show the world what you can do with the Ohmni Arm

The objective is to perform the useful tasks with the Ohmni Arm plus any additional 3d printed hardware/electronics that they build. These will all be open sourced on Kambria after the competition is over.

With the Ohmni coding framework for robotics - developers, researchers, and students can get the power of our telepresence platform in one unified package. Until now, truly open platforms for telepresence have not existed, and developers have been reinventing code with little or no reuse. With the Ohmni developer kit built on the proven technology used in our own robots, we enable faster, cheaper, and easier development of your telepresence applications.

About the Ohmni DevKit

The new Ohmni DevKit is based on the affordable, production-grade Ohmni telepresence robot and is designed for maximum hardware and software extensibility. Any developer can plug in custom hardware (such as Arduino-based electronics, servos, lights), as well as their own USB devices. The newly launched in-call Ohmni API makes it easy to remotely control these devices, with no networking, signaling, or cloud development required. With Ohmni coding framework for robotics, developers can build rich, interactive HTML5/CSS/JS overlays to Ohmni's browser-based calls that can send and receive commands in real time with Ohmni and your custom devices.

The Ohmni DevKit includes:

  • 1x Ohmni Dev Edition - ROS dev libraries and modules preinstalled
  • 3D printed mounting bracket kit - attach your devices anywhere on Ohmni
  • Arduino-based flashlight sample module and reference design
  • Arduino power + serial adapter kit
  • Arduino libraries and reference code for packet bus

Advantages of new Ohmni DevKit:

  • Easy to extend Ohmni’s browser telepresence UI with your own custom overlays
  • No need for network development, infrastructure, or signaling
  • Leverage Ohmni’s hardware accelerated HD audio/video codecs
  • Full ROS support
  • Plug and play support for 3rd party electronics and hardware, i.e. Arduino
  • Ability to add your own USB devices, such as depth cameras
  • Ohmni OS built on top of open frameworks like Android 7.1
  • Modifiable, standard Linux kernel makes it easy to add device support
  • Easy to build and deploy using Ohmni cloud robotics development framework
  • Customizable height between 4'8" and 4' tall for natural interaction

Contestants

Interested? Register now. Submit your idea, form a team, or if you need more inspiration, look for a concept you want to explore with other innovators. Explore our blog for additional inspiration, spark your creativity, generate bold solutions or find talented team members.

Step 1 - Registration, Ideation, and Team Formation June 13 - August 8

1. Work alone or as part of a team (6 people maximum)

2. Register your team here and register a Codebase for your team

3. Submit your application before August 8 by committing the proposal into your Codebase

Step 2 - Coaching and Collaboration June 13 - August 8

After momentum has been built over the course of the first four weeks, coaches will begin to provide feedback and encouragement to teams to refine ideas and prepare for submission.

Step 3 - Judging June 13 - August 8

1. Judging Round 1 (August 8 - 15): Private Vote by Kambria expert team

2. Judging Round 2 (August 15 - 24): Public Vote by Kambria community

Step 4 - Winners Selected September

In September, winners will be selected

Test assign judges

Vote will be awarded for creativity and technical difficulty. The following matrix will be used to vote for the project

1. Impact to the society and Kambria community

2. Creativity of the developer

3. Technical difficulty of the solution

4. Completeness of the Solution & KDNA

Eligibility

  • Participation in the Challenge, and as such, the awarding of prizes, is limited to individuals who are legal adults (and can prove this via identity documents) or, in the event that the person is a minor, have provided permission from their parent or guardian via the parental permission form, duly completed and signed.
  • At the time of registration, participants must certify that they fulfil the aforementioned criteria. The delivery of prizes to winners will be conditional upon participants’ ability to justify their compliance with the aforementioned conditions.

Participation

  • Participants may make submissions at any point during which the Challenge is open (June 13 - August 8)
  • Participants may make as many distinct submissions as they like. Duplicate submissions will be disregarded.

Team Size

  • Team size should be between one and six users on the platform.
  • Individuals may submit ideas on behalf of a larger, pre-existing team.

Intellectual Property

  • The output and other information or documentation submitted by participants will be openly licensed and published in Kambria Codebase.
  • The solutions proposed under the Challenge remain the property of the participants. Challenge partners are not authorized to commercialize or implement these solutions without receiving prior consent from the appropriate teams.
  • All the participants guarantee that they are the exclusive authors of the content (ideas, creations, images and more generally any document) of the projects and that these do not violate, directly and/or indirectly, or incorporate any element that may be the object of a property right of a third party, without any limit of territory.

Selection of Winners

  • The winning solution will be selected after 2 round of votes
    • Judging round 1
      Global expert from Kambria will utilize KambriaVote to vote for 10 teams
    • Judging round 2
      The public will do another round of vote for the 10 selected solution. The solution with highest number of votes from both public and expert team will be awarded
